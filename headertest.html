Assessment tool

Bronchoscopy

The Bronchoscopy assessment tool allows standardized assessment of clinical bronchoscopy procedures.

× Rater manual
This assessment tool consisting of 16 items, rates the surgeon performing a clinical bronchoscopy throughout the entire procedure. The 16 items cover:
  • Administration of Local anesthesia (item 1)
  • The ability to move around the bronchial tree in a gentle and competent way (items 2 & 9 - 11)
  • Demonstrating anatomical knowledge (performing a systematic and thorough procedure with visualization of all 19 bronchial segments) (items 3 – 8)
  • Ability to describe the procedure and the findings (items 12 - 16)
After completion of the procedure, the physician fills out the Thompson bronchitis index and writes a general description of the procedure and findings.
The items 1 – 11 are to be rated on a 3-point-scale.
When assessing the physician’s Thompson bronchitis index, it should be compared with your own bronchitis index for the patient as a gold standard. Total agreement scores 2 points, one point off scores 1 point and more than one point off scores zero points.
Item 16 is assessed by reading the physician’s general description of the procedure and rating it on a 5-point-scale.
The maximum score from the 16 items is 34 points.
Furthermore, you can assess the time needed to perform the procedure and divide it with the total score to determine the physician’s quality score (score/min).
To ensure the most valid results from this assessment tool, you should aim to complete the assessment as close to the performance of the surgery as possible, ideally during the procedure, to combat recall bias.
This assessment should be performed without regards for the surgeon’s education level and previous experience. It is therefore to be expected that the inexperienced surgeon will score relatively lower than the experienced. That way it will be possible to keep track of any progression in the surgeon’s technical skills.
